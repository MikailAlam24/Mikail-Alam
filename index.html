Houston, TX

Discover the vibrant heart of Texas in Houston, where Southern hospitality meets urban diversity. Home to the pioneering NASA Johnson Space Center and the world's largest rodeo, this dynamic city invites you to explore its rich tapestry of cultures, cuisine, and cutting-edge innovation. Whether you're stargazing from the Space Center or savoring flavors from around the globe, Houston promises unforgettable experiences and a warm Texas welcome. Come see why Houston is a city like no other!

Fun Facts

  • Houston is nicknamed "Space City" because it's home to NASA's Johnson Space Center, where Mission Control Center is located. This has been the heart of NASAâ€™s astronaut training and flight control since the 1960s.
  • The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo is the largest rodeo in the world. It attracts millions of visitors each year and features concerts, a carnival, livestock show, and, of course, lots of rodeo action. It started in 1932 and has grown into a massive event that significantly impacts the local culture and economy.
  • Houston is one of the most diverse cities in the United States. It has no racial or ethnic majority, with large Hispanic, African American, and Asian populations. This diversity is reflected in its food, culture, and festivals, making it a vibrant place to live and visit.

Tourist Attractions

  1. Visit the Space Center Houston: As the official visitor center of NASA Johnson Space Center, this is a must-see for anyone interested in space and science. It offers an incredible look behind the scenes of America's space program. Attractions include tram tours of NASA's facilities, exhibits of spacecraft and moon rocks, and interactive displays that simulate space missions.
  2. Explore the Houston Museum District: This area features 19 museums, many of which offer free admission. Highlights include the Houston Museum of Natural Science, the Museum of Fine Arts, and the Children's Museum. This district provides an enriching experience with extensive collections of art, science, history, and culture.
  3. Stroll through the Houston Zoo: Located in Hermann Park, the Houston Zoo is home to over 6,000 animals from 900 species. It's a fun and educational experience for visitors of all ages, offering close-up encounters with everything from marine life in the aquarium to exotic creatures in the African forest exhibit.
